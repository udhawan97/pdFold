<Callout variant="whentouse">
	You need to sign a PDF and send back a finished copy — either a quick visual mark or a verifiable digital signature.
</Callout>

Orifold has two kinds of signing, and they answer different needs. Open the **Signatures** palette and pick the method that matches what your recipient actually requires.

## Which one do I need?

| | Visual signature | Digital signature |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Palette methods** | Draw · Type · Initials | Digital |
| **What it is** | A picture of your signature placed on the page | A cryptographic signature bound to the file with a certificate |
| **Proves the file is unchanged?** | No | Yes — any edit after signing breaks it |
| **Proves who signed?** | No | Yes, if the certificate is trusted |
| **Good for** | Approvals, internal forms, "sign here" lines | Contracts, filings, anything requiring verification |

<PetTip who="ori">
	Pick deliberately. A drawn mark is a picture; a digital signature is math. Sending the first where the second is required is the most common signing mistake.
</PetTip>

## Add a visual signature (Draw, Type, or Initials)

1. Open the **Signatures** palette from the annotation toolbar.
2. Choose **Draw** (trackpad or cursor), **Type** (styled from your name), or **Initials**.
3. Place the mark on the page and resize it as needed.
4. Export the signed PDF.

<Media poster="/Orifold/assets/gifs/sign-document-workflow.svg" alt="Three steps of signing: draw a signature, place and resize it on the signature line, then export the signed PDF." caption="Drawing, placing, and exporting a visual signature — entirely on your Mac." spec="4–6s · draw a signature → place on the line → export" />

<Callout variant="tip">
	No e-sign service, no upload — your mark is drawn and placed entirely on your Mac and never touches a server.
</Callout>

<Callout variant="note">
	A drawn, typed, or initialed mark is a *visual* signature — a picture on the page. It doesn't cryptographically prove who signed or that the file is unchanged. When that proof matters, use a digital signature below.
</Callout>

## Apply a digital signature

A digital signature binds a cryptographic proof to the file using a **Digital ID** (a certificate + private key). Orifold produces a standards-based PAdES signature entirely on your Mac. The optional trusted timestamp is the one step that goes online: a timestamp authority is sent a SHA-256 hash of the signature bytes — never your document — and returns a signed proof of time.

1. Open the **Signatures** palette and choose **Digital**.
2. Provide a Digital ID:
   - **Import .p12 / CA-issued Digital ID** — a certificate file you already have.
   - **Choose Keychain Digital ID** — an identity already in your macOS Keychain.
   - **Generate self-signed ID** — created on the spot, no cost (see the caveat below).
3. Optionally enable a **timestamp** so the signature records a trusted time (requires an internet connection — this is the only feature in Orifold that makes a network request).
4. Click **Sign & Export…** and choose where to save.

<Callout variant="note">
	**Self-signed IDs show as "identity not verified" in other viewers.** The signature is cryptographically valid and the document is fully protected, but because no certificate authority vouches for a self-signed ID, viewers like Adobe Acrobat report "the signer's identity is unknown" until you (or your recipient) manually trust it. For signatures others must verify automatically, use a CA-issued Digital ID. See **Getting a Digital ID** in the palette.
</Callout>

<Details summary="What Orifold produces, technically">

Orifold writes an **append-only incremental update** with a detached CMS signature over a correct `ByteRange` — the original bytes are never rewritten, so a valid signature means the whole document is intact.

- **Standard:** PAdES (`ETSI.CAdES.detached`), SHA-256.
- **With a timestamp:** PAdES B-T — an RFC-3161 token is requested from a TSA (a free default, or your chosen provider) and embedded. If the TSA is unreachable, export continues as PAdES B-B and warns you.
- **Verified with:** `pdfsig` (poppler) and `openssl asn1parse` report *Signature is Valid* and *Total document signed*.
- **Out of scope today:** long-term validation (LTV / OCSP / CRL embedding).

For the engineering detail, see the [Developer FAQ](/Orifold/developers/faq/) and [the engines](/Orifold/developers/engines/).

</Details>

## What you should see

A visual mark appears exactly where you placed it and travels with the exported PDF. A digital signature produces a signed file that verifies as intact in any PAdES-aware viewer — with a trusted-identity indicator if your Digital ID is CA-issued.
