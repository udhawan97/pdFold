Orifold ships with a small built-in companion that offers short, useful hints for moments that matter — exporting, saving, and anything that needs your attention. It's designed to stay out of your document's way: a small, quiet chip in the corner, with hints that keep a safe distance from your page and collapse to a small badge rather than crowd a busy window.

<Figure src="/Orifold/assets/screenshots/companion-gami-ori.svg" alt="Gami the origami Bernedoodle and Ori the origami Siberian shown side by side in the corner of a workspace." caption="Gami (left) and Ori (right), the Orifold companions." />

## Two very different personalities

Pick **Gami · Orifold Guide** (an origami Bernedoodle — floppy black ears, a cream blaze and chest, a calm wag) or **Ori** (an origami Siberian — a plush cream ruff, tufted lynx-tip ears, a long tail that wraps composedly around her paws) on first launch. Switch anytime from the app menu or the companion's own popover.

## Seeing the detail

Both companions are folded paper with real depth — layered tails, folded ears, a ruff you can see the layers of. On first launch, your companion shows off at full size for a few seconds so you can watch the fold before it settles into its corner; hover it any time afterward to bring that detail back. Reduce Motion skips the showcase and keeps the companion at its resting size throughout.

They're siblings folded from the same sheet of paper — different mothers' patterns, same Origami — and their voices are deliberately opposite:

- **Gami** is warm, playful, loyal, and encouraging. He moves toward you: *"Exporting? I can walk you through the options."*
- **Ori** is curious, clever, elegant, calm, and charmingly bossy. She lets you come to her, noticing things before she comments on them: *"Gami would chase the cursor. I prefer strategy."*

## What they help with

Gami and Ori speak up for hero moments — exporting, saving, first-time use of a feature, and warnings — rather than narrating every click. The rest of the time they just pulse quietly to acknowledge what you did, so the tips stay occasional instead of chatty. Ori also waits a little longer than Gami before a hover tip appears — she notices you before she speaks.

## Switching companions

If you haven't discovered the popover on your own by your third session, a small one-time card near the chip introduces both companions and points at the switcher — *"Gami keeps things playful. Ori supervises with taste."* It shows at most once: opening the popover or switching species yourself retires it immediately, and dismissing it retires it too.

## Quieting the tips, or turning them off entirely

Click the companion chip to open its popover:

- **Hide Gami Tips** — keeps the companion visible but stops hint bubbles; useful if you'd rather explore on your own.
- **Show Orifold Buddy** (app menu) — hides the companion entirely. Everything else in Orifold works exactly the same either way.
