Orifold ships with a small built-in companion that offers short, useful hints for moments that matter — exporting, saving, and anything that needs your attention. It's designed to stay out of your document's way: a small, quiet chip in the corner, with hints that keep a safe distance from your page and collapse to a small badge rather than crowd a busy window.

<Figure src="/Orifold/assets/screenshots/companion-gami-ori.svg" alt="Gami the origami Bernedoodle and Ori the origami cat shown side by side in the corner of a workspace." caption="Gami (left) and Ori (right), the Orifold companions." />

## Choosing a companion

Pick **Gami · Orifold Guide** (an origami Bernedoodle — floppy black ears, a cream blaze and chest, a calm wag) or **Ori** (the origami cat) on first launch. Gami is warm and encouraging in a quiet, professional way; Ori is clever, composed, and quietly in charge. Switch anytime from the app menu or the companion's own popover.

## What they help with

Gami and Ori speak up for hero moments — exporting, saving, first-time use of a feature, and warnings — rather than narrating every click. The rest of the time they just pulse quietly to acknowledge what you did, so the tips stay occasional instead of chatty.

## Quieting the tips, or turning them off entirely

Click the companion chip to open its popover:

- **Hide Gami Tips** — keeps the companion visible but stops hint bubbles; useful if you'd rather explore on your own.
- **Show Orifold Buddy** (app menu) — hides the companion entirely. Everything else in Orifold works exactly the same either way.
